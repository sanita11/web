About Rosé

Roseanne Park, known by her stage name Rose, is a singer and dancer from New Zealand, who is a member of the South Korean girl group BLACKPINK. She was born on February 11, 1997, in Auckland, New Zealand, and later moved to Melbourne, Australia, with her family. Rose was scouted by YG Entertainment at the age of 16, and joined the company as a trainee in 2012. She trained for several years in singing, dancing, and other aspects of performance, before making her debut as a member of BLACKPINK in 2016. Rose is known for her powerful vocals, emotive performances, and unique fashion sense. She has been praised for her ability to convey emotion through her singing, and has been compared to other great vocalists in the K-Pop industry. She has also become a style icon, known for her edgy and eclectic fashion choices both on and off stage. Outside of her work with BLACKPINK, Rose has collaborated with other artists on various projects, including a duet with Korean singer G-Dragon, and an appearance on the soundtrack of the Korean drama "It's Okay to Not Be Okay". She has also performed solo at various events and concerts, showcasing her musical and performance skills.

As the main vocalist of the group, Rosé is known for her powerful vocals and emotional performances. She has also pursued a solo career, releasing her debut solo single "On the Ground" in March 2021.
